```markmap
# You had better learn modals!
## introduction
- modals are auxiliary verbs
- express a speaker's attitude and the strength of that attitude
- example: "You should visit Prague."
- The simple form of a verb goes after a modal

## Using should
### has multiple meanings
- express certainty
"He should be here by five o'clock"
- be a substitute for the conditional word `if`
"Should you need help, just ask me."
- give suggestions and friendly advice
"You should apply for that job"
"You should try that new restaurant"

### past form of the modal `should`
- should have + the past participle
- "I should have known better than to trust him."
- to express regret, or negative feeling about the past

## Using ought to 

## Using had better
### stronger than should and ought to
- carries an indriect threat
"You had better finish the report."
- it could express a sense of urgency 
"Your plane is leaving! You had better run!"

## Modals in rapid speech
- casual conversation
- they are shortened and offten fall on unstressed syllables
- "I should have been listening to what she had to say."
"I shoulda been listenin'ta what she had t' say."
- ought to -> otta
- You had better -> You'd better
```
